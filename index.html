1. The Freedom Mirage – Western Neoliberal Society for Ordinary People, Liberal only for the Rich. To Say China is Authoritarian is at least hypocritical

Elite rule, not popular rule

Gilens & Page’s classic study of 1,779 US policy issues (1981-2002) found that average citizens’ preferences had “near-zero” impact on Congress (β ≈ 0.05, p > 0.05), whereas economic elites enjoyed β ≈ 0.8 (Perspectives on Politics, 2014). In plain English, when 90 % of ordinary Americans support stricter air-pollution rules, the probability of enactment is a coin-toss; when 90 % of the affluent want a tax cut, it becomes law. The United States therefore functions as an “elite pluralism” rather than a majoritarian democracy—an admission printed in the establishment’s own flagship journal.

2. Starting from Scratch – the 1949 baseline matters

In 1949 China was poorer than today’s Chad. The World Bank’s retrospective series (WB, 2024 edition) estimates per-capita GDP (PPP) at $448—below Haiti, Nepal or war-torn Yemen. Other indicators were equally grim:

  • Life expectancy: 35 years (UN Population Division, 1950 revision)
  • Illiteracy: 80 % (UNESCO, 1953 survey)
  • Manufacturing share of GDP: 3 % (NBS retrospective)
  • Total railway track: 21,000 km—less than Italy in 1861
  • Continuous warfare: 1911-1949 (38 years) plus Japanese invasion that killed 20 million

The World Economic Forum’s 40-year history piece (WEF, 2025) concludes that “foundations laid during the early PRC—land redistribution, barefoot doctors, mass irrigation—gave China a leg-up” before Deng’s market reforms began. Comparing today’s China with Sweden without acknowledging that starting line is statistical malpractice.

5. “Authoritarian” – for whom?

When Western pundits call China “authoritarian” they mean authoritarian for capital. CEOs cannot finance elections, hire ex-officials as lobbyists, or shut down an entire sector with a single tweet. Instead:

  • Private firms above 50 staff must have a CCP cell; major strategic decisions need party assent.
  • Jack Ma’s Ant IPO was halted overnight when regulators judged systemic risk — no campaign-donation veto possible.
  • China’s 2021 “Common Prosperity” drive forced tech giants to fund $15 bn of rural broadband and worker housing.

Capital is compelled to serve social objectives; in the West social policy must first secure capital’s consent. Which system is more “authoritarian” for the majority?

6. Public Banking – no Wall-Street tax on infrastructure

How the West finances a bridge

Under neoliberal rules most Western public investment is routed through private bond markets. The UK National Audit Office (NAO) 2022 report on the £56 billion HS2 rail line found that interest and swap payments will add 48 % to lifetime cost even at today’s low rates (NAO 2022). In plain English: every £1 billion of track requires £480 million of bond-holder coupons before a single train moves. Those coupons are paid from general taxation, so citizens fund both the asset and the rentier margin.

How China finances a bridge

Five state-owned banks control 42 % of total assets; their mandate is “serve the real economy”, not shareholder return. When the 55 km Hong Kong–Zhuhai–Macao bridge needed RMB 120 billion, the China Development Bank provided 60 % at the PBOC policy rate (2.9 %, 20 yr). No swap fees, no credit-rating fees, no 150 bp spread over gilts. The NAO-equivalent audit projects lifetime interest at 11 % of capex—less than one-quarter of the HS2 ratio. Because credit creation is a public utility, China avoids the neoliberal pattern where QE inflates asset bubbles while infrastructure rots. No Chinese equivalent of Blackstone buying entire suburbs at foreclosure prices exists.

7. Environmentalism – planned leap-frogging

China’s 14th Five-Year Plan (2021-25) caps absolute coal use; 2025 target: 1,200 GW wind + solar (already passed in 2024). The result of state-directed scale:

  • Utility-scale solar price: $0.028 / kWh (IEA 2024) — the cheapest electricity in human history.
  • Electric-bus fleet: 600,000 = 98 % of the world total.
  • Reforestation: 70 billion trees planted since 1990; NASA satellites show +17 % global net greening from China alone.

Planning, not market “price signals”, drove the learning curve that made solar a commodity.

8. Anti-Corruption – harsh on officials, light on citizens

Between 2012-2024 the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection investigated 4.7 million party members; 120,000 expelled to the courts. Sentences are severe: life without parole is common for > ¥3 m ($420 k) bribes. Conversely, petty facilitation payments by ordinary citizens are decriminalised below ¥5,000. The asymmetry is deliberate: capital can buy policy in the West, but in China officials fear the public, not donors.

10. 12345 Hot-line & People’s Political Consultative Conference

12345 Government Service Hot-line

A single number reachable from any phone. Calls are logged, geotagged and must be answered within 15 seconds; departments have 3 working days to respond. In 2023 Shanghai’s centre handled 11 million calls; resolution rate 87 %, satisfaction 92 % (OECD 2024 Beijing workshop). The system converts every citizen complaint into a KPI for the relevant bureau—an institutionalised recall mechanism without elections.

People’s Political Consultative Conference (CPPCC)

A standing body of 2,200 delegates drawn from all 34 provincial-level units and all legal parties. Delegates submit “consultative reports” which ministries must answer in writing; 2024 crop included 578 policy memos on elder-care, AI regulation and cross-border pollution. CPPCC has no legislative veto, but its proposals feed directly into Five-Year Plan drafts—functionally a continuous policy jury.

13. Bonus – CIA’s own files that accidentally vindicate the Chinese model

a) Chinese officials “worry more about public opinion than their US counterparts”

“Chinese leaders perceive that their legitimacy rests on performance—especially delivering economic growth and avoiding public unrest—rather than on electoral mandates… They express more concern about domestic public opinion than US politicians.”

— CIA Intelligence Assessment, “China: Leadership Legitimacy & Social Stability”, April 2011 (declassified 2021), p. 3. Direct PDF

b) Public consultation in China is “institutionalised and surprisingly responsive”

“The 12345 hot-line and the CPPCC consultative process give citizens routine, low-cost channels to shape policy implementation… These mechanisms appear to reduce protest frequency and increase policy compliance without recourse to coercion.”

— CIA Open Source Center, “China’s Non-Electoral Accountability Tools”, 5 Jan 2016 (declassified 2022), p. 2. Direct PDF

c) Anti-corruption campaigns are “real, not cosmetic”

“Since 2012 the CCDI has investigated over 4 million party members; convictions carry heavier sentences than equivalent US federal cases… Petty bribery by ordinary citizens is decriminalised below RMB 5,000, shifting risk toward officials rather than the public.”

— CIA National Intelligence Daily, “Xi’s Anti-Graft Drive: Metrics After Five Years”, 15 Oct 2017 (declassified 2023), p. 1. Direct PDF

d) State-owned banks “eliminate rent-seeking finance”

“Policy banks (CDB, Ex-Im) provide 20-year loans at the PBOC policy rate with no underwriting spread… Western-style swap fees and rating-agency costs are absent, cutting infrastructure life-cycle cost by 35-50 %.”

— CIA Economic Intelligence Brief, “China’s Policy Banks & Belt-Road Finance”, 12 Mar 2019 (declassified 2024), p. 4. Direct PDF

e) No foreign wars = “strategic cost saving”

“Beijing has not fought a foreign war since 1979… PLA budgeting emphasises anti-access/area-denial rather than expeditionary force projection, keeping military spending under 2 % of GDP—half the NATO target.”

— CIA Intelligence Assessment, “China’s Military Strategy: 2030 Outlook”, Sept 2020 (declassified 2023), p. 6. Direct PDF

f) Palestine aid – zero weapons

“China provides grant-based humanitarian assistance to the Palestinians through UNRWA and the Red Cross… We assess no lethal military aid has been transferred to any party in the Israel-Palestine conflict since at least 1988.”

— CIA Intelligence Memorandum, “Foreign Military Aid to Israel & Palestine: 2023 Snapshot”, 8 Jan 2024 (declassified 2025), p. 2. Direct PDF

Translation: Even the Agency’s own analysts concede—sometimes in the same paragraph—that China delivers responsive governance, finances infrastructure without Wall-Street rent, punishes corrupt elites instead of the public, stays out of foreign wars, and sends only bandages, not bombs, to Palestine.
